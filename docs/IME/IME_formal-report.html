III. Alleged Mechanism of Injury & History of Present Illness

Mr. Doe reports that on 06/01/2024, while employed as a delivery driver with ABC Logistics, he was lifting a heavy box from the floor of his truck when he felt an acute onset of lower back pain, described as a “sharp pull” across the lumbar region.

He states that symptoms began immediately, with difficulty straightening upright and pain radiating into the left posterior thigh. He denies a specific fall or direct trauma, but notes that the event occurred during a twisting motion while lifting.

In subsequent medical visits, the mechanism is generally described consistently as a lifting and twisting injury at work on the above date, with some variation in details regarding the weight of the box and whether radiating symptoms were present at onset.

IV. Relevant Prior Medical History

Prior records document intermittent episodes of mechanical low back pain dating back to 2018. These episodes were typically self-limited, treated conservatively with non-steroidal anti-inflammatory medications and home exercises. There is no documentation of prior radicular symptoms or prior lumbar spine surgery.

Imaging prior to the 2024 event is limited; there is no lumbar MRI on record before the alleged date of injury. Routine primary care visits note mild lumbar spondylosis on plain radiographs in 2021.

VII. Current Complaints & Functional Status

As of the most recent documentation, Mr. Doe reports persistent low back pain rated at approximately 4–6/10 with prolonged standing or lifting. He denies current radicular pain. He reports difficulty with repetitive bending and lifting tasks but is able to perform basic activities of daily living with pacing and modification.

He has returned to modified work duties involving primarily light lifting and reduced bending. There is no documentation of progressive neurologic deficit.

VIII. Medico-Legal Factors to Consider (Informational Only)
Factors Supporting Causation
  • Clear temporal relationship between lifting incident and onset of acute low back pain.
  • Immediate reporting of injury to employer and presentation to Emergency Department.
  • Initial clinical findings consistent with lumbar strain after lifting injury.
Factors Weakening Causation
  • Documented history of intermittent low back pain prior to 2024, with degenerative changes noted on prior imaging.
  • Current MRI findings predominantly degenerative in nature (facet arthropathy, mild disc protrusions).
  • No clear imaging evidence of acute structural disruption attributable solely to the 06/01/2024 event.
Pre-existing vs Post-Injury Analysis

Prior records suggest a baseline of episodic mechanical low back pain associated with degenerative changes. The 06/01/2024 lifting event appears to represent an acute exacerbation on a background of pre-existing degenerative lumbar disease, with no convincing evidence of a new structural lesion beyond what would be expected from age-related changes.
